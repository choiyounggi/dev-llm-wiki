# Choosing a Primary Key

## When this applies

You are creating a table and must pick its primary key, or evaluating whether an
existing key choice is causing problems (bloat, hot inserts, leaking ids).

## Do this

1. Default to a **surrogate key** (generated id). Keep natural candidates
   (email, SKU, national id) as `UNIQUE` constraints instead — natural keys change
   with business rules, and a PK change cascades through every referencing table.
2. Pick the surrogate type by exposure and topology:

| Case | Do |
|------|----|
| Internal table, ids not exposed or enumeration is harmless | `BIGINT` sequence/identity — smallest, fastest, index-friendly. Use `BIGINT`, not `INT`: retrofitting a full table under traffic is expensive |
| Ids exposed in URLs/APIs where enumeration or volume inference matters | Random-looking id. Prefer UUIDv7 (RFC 9562): random enough to not enumerate, time-ordered so B-tree inserts stay append-ish |
| Ids generated by clients/offline devices or across shards before insert | UUID (v7 when generation-time ordering is acceptable, v4 otherwise) |
| MySQL/InnoDB specifically | The PK is the clustered index — the table is physically ordered by it, and every secondary index stores the PK. Random PKs (UUIDv4) scatter inserts and bloat all secondary indexes; keep the clustered PK sequential (auto_increment or UUIDv7) |

3. Store UUIDs in native `uuid` / `BINARY(16)` types, not as text — half the size,
   correct comparisons.

## Edge cases

| Case | Then |
|------|------|
| Junction (many-to-many) table | Composite PK of the two FKs is standard; add the reverse-order index for queries entering from the other side |
| Multi-tenant tables | Lead unique constraints and hot indexes with `tenant_id`; whether the PK itself is composite (`tenant_id, id`) follows your sharding/RLS plan, and it must be decided before data accumulates |
| UUIDv7 timestamp prefix leaks creation time | When creation time is itself sensitive, use v4 and accept the insert-locality cost |
| Sequence ids leak business volume (order ids) | Expose a separate public identifier; keep the sequence internal |

## Instead of

| If you are about to | Do this instead | Why |
|---------------------|-----------------|-----|
| Use email/username/SKU as the PK | Surrogate PK + `UNIQUE` on the natural key | Natural keys change; PK changes cascade through FKs everywhere |
| Default every table to UUIDv4 "for scale" | BIGINT identity, or UUIDv7 where ids must be non-sequential | v4's randomness costs insert locality and index size with no benefit for internal tables |
